Current of forecast hospital use for COVID-19 patients in the Ottawa area. Created by the team behind ProjectBigLife.ca at the Ottawa Hospital and the University of Ottawa with input and data from Ottawa Public Health.

NOTE: Ottawa hospitalization data is missing from 2020-04-29 to 2020-05-04. Observed hospitalization data from The Ottawa Hospital will be used in the interim as the COVID-19 Ottawa Database is being developed by Ottawa Public Health.

Note regarding death projections. Death & ICU projections have been omitted from the website. With the overwhelming majority of COVID-19 related deaths occurring in residents of long-term care (LTC) or retirement homes, it is difficult to estimate COVID-19 related deaths in the hospital, as hospital death estimates are based on ICU probabilities. Many residents in LTC and retirement homes are in their last years of life and have advanced directives saying they (the resident) do not want advanced life support or transfer to the hospital if they have a life threatening illness. With the situation in LTC and retirement homes stabilizing, we hope to return these projections.

Observed Hospital census

Hospital census halving time of 13.4 days translates to current physical distancing effectiveness of approximately 82%.

Hospital census is the count of patients in all Ottawa hospitals at midnight on the previous day.

Doubling time refers to the time it takes for the number of hospitalized patients to double in number.

Halving time is the time it takes to halve the number of hospitalized patients. Doubling/halving time was calculated for each 7 day period (Mon-Sun).

Peak hospitalization projections

The forecast of 35 patients in hospital in Apr 2020 is based on the previous weeks’ physical distancing effectiveness of 82%.

Physical distancing is the reduction in close contact between people.

Physical distancing effectiveness is the amount that people without COVID-19 stay away from people who are infectious with COVID-19, compared to the time before COVID-19 control measures.

See More page for more information about physical distancing effectiveness.
